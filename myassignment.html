Marsh, Hazlewood to the fore as Australia lifts title

Allrounder’s three wickets burst and crucial runs complement pacer’s second ODI five-for as world champions beat West Indies by 58 runs Australia clinched the triangular One-Day International series in the Caribbean win a 58-run victory over West Indies on Sunday (June 26) evening at the Kensington Oval in Barbados. Australia’s victory was based on three crucial contributions: an incisive spell by Mitchell Marsh (10-1-32-3), who was later named Man of the Match, a late counter-attacking innings from Matthew Wade (57 not out in 52 balls) and Josh Hazlewood’s five-wicket haul (5 for 50). Their efforts enabled Australia to put up a competitive 270 for 9 before it bowled out West Indies for 212 in 45.4 overs.
